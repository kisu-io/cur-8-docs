By category

☕ Café chains (vs. specialty)

Katinat is the only true chain; Là Việt is a specialty single-origin roaster, not a chain. Grouping them together overstates CUR8's café exposure. High volume / low ticket (30–100K) applies to Katinat only.

🏪 Independent cafés

4 venues: R.A.C, Lalin's, Nghệ Thuật, Mach. Character-focused, loyal local customers, smaller footprint. Threat is individually low but collectively absorbs the "walk-in AM" market within 200m.

🍺 Coffee + beer hybrid — most similar to CUR8 AM→PM

2 venues: R.A.C, Carpe Diem. Day-to-night transition, dual revenue streams. Neither is dominant — opportunity for CUR8 to own this positioning with stronger PM execution (Sound Lab + dedicated program).

🎵 Pub / lounge / club

3 venues: Home Pub, FETTI, The Arth. Night-focused, higher ticket (200–500K), entertainment-driven. Strong PM competition. Note: FETTI and The Arth pull the weekend party persona (P4) that CUR8 should not aggressively chase.

🎧 Listening bar / audiophile — CUR8 Sound Lab peer set

3 venues in HCMC: Standing Bar, Nest, Mono. None has a bookable listening room at CUR8's Sound Lab size. This is the clearest white-space structural claim CUR8 can make city-wide.

White-space opportunities

1. Day-to-night with a dedicated listening room

R.A.C and Carpe Diem attempt the AM→PM pivot but neither has an audiophile room. Standing Bar / Nest / Mono have sound but don't open AM. CUR8 combining both is structurally hard to copy.

2. Retail + F&B integration (vinyl shop)

No competitor in 1 km (and zero listening-bar peers city-wide) sells vinyl or merch in-store. CUR8 retail stream is genuinely novel in HCMC.

3. Bookable listening room — rare globally, unseen locally

Most listening rooms (Spiritland, Brilliant Corners, In Sheep's Clothing) are walk-in. Sound Lab as a bookable program unit lets CUR8 monetize high-margin private hires on weeknights when walk-in PM demand is weakest.

Competitive risks

1. Saturated café market. Katinat + Là Việt + independents already capture AM walk-in. CUR8 cafe daypart must differentiate on product (specialty roast + signature items) not convenience.

2. Fierce PM competition. Home Pub / FETTI / The Arth are established; Standing Bar / Nest / Mono own the audiophile niche. CUR8 needs clear messaging that "Sound Lab + open-air lounge + vinyl shop" is a combined promise, not three half-concepts.

3. Rent pressure. Phường Sài Gòn premium location means AM revenue is load-bearing for fixed-cost absorption. If AM misses, PM must overshoot — see Financial-Advisory risk mitigations.

4. New-entrant risk. At least two "coming soon" listening bars announced for D1 / D3 in 2026 (see Watchlist below). CUR8 opening in June gives a ≤6-month first-mover window before the scene gets crowded.

What these prove for CUR8

  • Multi-daypart (cafe → bar → listening) is validated globally.
  • A dedicated listening room creates a durable competitive moat.
  • Vietnamese-led music curation differentiates CUR8 from Japanese-pastiche clones in HCMC.
  • Sound Lab as a bookable program is rare globally — most listening rooms are walk-in.
  • Vinyl club / membership recurring revenue is virtually untapped in Vietnam.

Methodology — how we got these numbers

Each revenue estimate uses the following formula:

monthly_rev = seats × turns_per_day × avg_ticket × open_days × utilization
  • seats — observed capacity from field visits; photo evidence logged in Visits/.
  • turns_per_day — 1.2–1.8 for café (AM), 1.0–1.4 for listening/cocktail (PM), 1.8–2.4 for pub/club (PM peak).
  • avg_ticket — mid-point of the range above; cross-checked against menu photos.
  • open_days — 26 / 28 per month (closed Mon / Tue for some venues).
  • utilization — 0.55 weekday, 0.85 weekend (blended 0.65 except FETTI / Home Pub which skew weekend-heavy at 0.75).

Estimates are therefore order-of-magnitude (±30%), not precise revenue figures. They are useful for relative positioning, not for financial modelling. Do not cite these numbers in investor materials without the methodology footnote.

Response playbook — 3 scenarios

Scenario A — new listening bar opens within 500m before CUR8 launch

  • Week 0–2: field visit + menu photo + sound-system audit.
  • Week 2–4: re-position Sound Lab messaging — if the new entrant is walk-in, lean harder into bookable private; if they're program-curated, lean into daypart breadth.
  • Defer: any price increase on cocktails until 3 months post-opening.
  • Owner: Founder + Program curator · Trigger: Watchlist flips to "Now open".

Scenario B — The Arth / FETTI launches a listening or vinyl night

  • This is the biggest P4 → P3 bridging risk; do not match on price.
  • Response: accelerate vinyl-club membership launch (originally planned Q4 2026 → pull forward to opening month).
  • Commission 1–2 resident DJs with exclusivity clauses (not co-residencies with The Arth / FETTI).
  • Owner: Program curator · Trigger: IG announcement + ≥2-week program cadence confirmed.

Scenario C — Standing Bar opens a D1 satellite

  • Highest-risk scenario; removes CUR8's "convenience vs. D2" advantage for P3.
  • Response: shift Sound Lab positioning from convenience to program depth; publish 90-day forward calendar; deepen Vietnamese-curator identity.
  • Consider co-branded guest series with Standing Bar (coopetition) to neutralize rivalry.
  • Owner: Founder · Trigger: BSA-equivalent permit filing in D1 under Standing Bar entity (monitor quarterly).
