What CUR8 is

CUR8 is a multi-concept, multi-daypart venue in District 1, HCMC. One address. Several moods. A building that behaves differently as the sun moves.

Daypart / zone Program Where Positioning
AM 10:00–16:00 — Cafe Specialty cafe · remote-work · light food Trệt (GF) Third-wave coffee with an audiophile ear — Vietnamese beans, vinyl on low volume
PM 16:00–00:00 — Open-air lounge Cocktail program, small plates, deliberate music Lầu 2 front (open-air terrace under the Lầu 3/4 setback) Outdoor-feel drinks under ceiling fans — this is the "rooftop" daypart, just on Lầu 2
Sound Lab Dedicated hi-fi listening room (bookable) Lầu 1 Jazz-kissa format — rare pressings, tube amplification, seated-only sessions
Vinyl shop Curated record retail Lầu 2 rear (red-box footprint, enclosed) Sits behind the lounge; a destination for the music-forward customer, tied to the Sound Lab program

Back of house: office on Lầu 3, kitchen/BOH on Lầu 4. Both are set-back volumes over the Lầu 2 rear, leaving Lầu 2 front open-air.

The building operates as a single hospitality product with multiple access points, not multiple sub-brands.

Why the "cafe → bar → sound lab" stack

  1. Daypart arbitrage. A single fit-out earns revenue 14 hours a day. HCMC rent does not distinguish between AM and PM — we will.
  2. Audiophile DNA. Premium audio is the one thread that runs through every daypart. Morning cortados and midnight Negronis are served on the same speakers.
  3. Discovery moat. The Sound Lab is the feature the competition cannot copy without rebuilding their room — Jackalope, Stir, Mizuwari, Kanpai all have music but none have a dedicated listening room.

Target customer

  • AM (cafe) — creative professionals, designers, remote founders; 25–40; spend 150–250K on coffee + light lunch.
  • PM (open-air lounge) — expat finance / tech / design crowd, local high-earners, hotel guests from PC/District-1 luxury properties; 28–50; spend 450–750K on cocktails + plates. Also absorbs the old "rooftop" group-booking and Instagram-walk-in segments — same people, different level.
  • Sound Lab — music-forward early-adopters willing to pay cover or min-spend for seated listening sessions. Small but high-margin.
  • Vinyl shop — collectors + adjacent PM lounge guests browsing between drinks. Attach-rate to bar spend, not a standalone destination.

What CUR8 is not

  • Not a nightclub (no dance floor, no bottle service).
  • Not a Japanese jazz-kissa pastiche (we respect the format but we are Vietnamese and the program shows it).
  • Not a coworking cafe (WiFi tables removed at 16:00 every day).
  • Not a restaurant (kitchen is focused; we are drinks-led).

Key assumptions baked into the concept

  • The same guest can come at 11:00 for cortado + laptop and come back at 21:00 for Negroni + plates. Loyalty compounds across dayparts.
  • The Sound Lab operates on scarcity — 12 seats, bookable — and its brand halo sells the rest of the venue.
  • The Lầu 2 front open-air lounge is the social-media engine and the revenue engine at once (it absorbs what used to be split between "PM bar" and "rooftop"). Cafe is the retention engine. Sound Lab is the differentiator. Vinyl shop is the music credibility anchor that ties everything together.
