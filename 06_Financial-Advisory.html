Financial Advisory — 19 April 2026

Parent: Home · Source: 06_financial_advisory.html · Verdict: CONDITIONAL GO

Verdict

Conditional go. The concept is commercially viable. The financial model is directionally correct but materially optimistic. Three critical issues must be addressed before any external capital commitment; five recommendations strengthen the plan.

3 critical issues

1. Revenue optimism — 20–35% overstatement

  • Top-down build (100/125 seats × 160K/700K check × days) implicit realization ~58%
  • Bottom-up seat-turn simulation supports 12.0–13.8B Y1 only under favorable conditions
  • The 700K Thu–Sat avg check is per-seat per-evening, not per-turn — this conflation inflates the plan
  • Fix: rebuild the revenue model on a seat-turn framework with per-turn avg check 340–420K

2. Missing month-by-month cashflow with realistic ramp

  • Model assumes 2–3 month ramp; advisory view is 6 months
  • Shape of Y1 matters more than total Y1 — working capital runway depends on monthly shape
  • Fix: produce month-by-month cash flow showing 6-month ramp, identifying the month of lowest cash balance

3. Construction budget scenarios not reconciled

  • V1 (2.71B) vs V2 (4.01B) gap of 1.3B is material
  • Sound Lab acoustic scope is the biggest delta and the most important differentiator
  • Fix: commit to a mid-case (~3.2B), reserve 990M capex headroom for FF&E + contingency

5 recommendations

  1. Stress-test avg check at 340K and 390K per-turn — this is the base-case and downside variable
  2. Rebuild revenue on seat-turn framework (see 04_Operations/Reports/03_Daypart-Simulation)
  3. Add month-by-month cashflow for Y1 reflecting 6-month ramp
  4. Add competitive-response scenarios — what if Kanpai expands, Jackalope upgrades, new entrant arrives
  5. Separate the Sound Lab P&L — it should carry its own economics, not be buried in PM bar revenue

3-scenario analysis

Scenario Y1 revenue Y1 EBITDA % Payback
Base (model-as-written) 13.83B 24.2% 24–30 mo
Advisory base (seat-turn + 6mo ramp + 390K check) 12.5B 20.5% 36–42 mo
Downside (slow ramp + 340K check + cafe underperforms) 10.1B 13.5% 60+ mo
Upside (fast ramp + 420K check + cafe retention) 15.4B 28.0% 18–24 mo

6 risk mitigations

  1. Pre-engage PCCC consultant week 1 — schedule protection
  2. Lock acoustic spec by week 2 — prevent late scope creep
  3. Long-lead equipment ordered now — Sound Lab, espresso, combi oven
  4. Phased hiring per 04_Operations/Reports/04_HR-Rampup — prevent labor over-hire
  5. Profit-share for Bar Lead + Kitchen Lead — retention
  6. Bridge facility arranged for month 4–6 in advance — in case revenue ramp is slow

6 tripwires (when to pause)

# Trigger Action
1 Month 3 revenue < 50% of model Pause capex, freeze hiring
2 Month 6 EBITDA negative Bridge funding conversation
3 Month 9 revenue < 75% of model Restructure — menu, labor, program
4 Construction cost > 110% of budget Freeze FF&E premium, defer rooftop upgrade
5 2 license rejections Operational pause, owner-level meeting
6 Loss of Bar Lead or Kitchen Lead in first 90 days Emergency hiring protocol

  2. Reconcile construction scenarios — required before signing main contractor contract

None of these blocks starting construction today, but all three must be resolved in the next 3–4 weeks.
