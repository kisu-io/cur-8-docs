Revenue Plan

Parent: Home · Source: 04_operations/revenue_plan.html · Related: PnL-Operations, 01_Planning/Unit-Economics, Reports/03_Daypart-Simulation

Two-block daily revenue model

CUR8's revenue plan splits the day into two operating blocks with distinct economics, staffing, and menu programs.

Block A — AM 10:00–16:00 (cafe daypart)

Variable Sun–Wed Thu–Sat
Active seats 100 100
Avg check 160K 200K (slight uplift on weekend brunch)
Bills/day ~120 ~150
Daily revenue ~16M ~24M

Block B — PM 16:00–01:00 (bar daypart)

Variable Sun–Wed Thu–Sat
Active seats 100 (same room reconfigured) 125
Avg check 280K 700K
Bills/day ~85 ~120
Daily revenue ~24M ~84M

Monthly run-rate: ~2,100M VND. The model uses a more conservative 1,215M (see PnL-Operations) accounting for ramp, seasonality, and realization. The implied realization factor is ~58%.

Variable revenue programs

Program Cadence Revenue uplift assumption
Sound Lab listening session 2 nights/week (Wed, Sat) +10M/session, ~80M/month
DJ Fri–Sat rooftop weekly +5M/night, ~40M/month
Private hire (full venue) 1–2/month 25–60M/event
Tasting menu collab w/ guest chef quarterly 30M/event
Vinyl listening club membership monthly fee 50–80 members × 500K = 25–40M/month

Realization risk

  • Block B Thu–Sat single-handedly generates 60% of weekly revenue. Concentration risk.
  • Block A Sun–Wed generates only ~13% of weekly revenue. The cafe carries thin margins; if it underperforms, fixed costs are exposed.
  • Weather kills rooftop revenue during May–Oct monsoon — assume 30% wet days.
  • Sound Lab is brand-driving but volume-limited.
