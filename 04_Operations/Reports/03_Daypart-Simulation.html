Report 03 — Daypart Simulation

Parent: Home · Source: 04_operations/reports/03_daypart_simulation.html · Related: ../Revenue-Plan, ../../01_Planning/Unit-Economics, ../../06_Financial-Advisory

Purpose

The finance model (see ../../01_Planning/Finance-Model) builds revenue top-down from seats × avg check × days. The daypart simulation rebuilds revenue bottom-up from seat-turns to stress-test the plan.

This is the core analytic ../../06_Financial-Advisory asks for before external commitments.

Seat-turn framework

Revenue = Seats × Turns × Avg-check-per-turn × Operating-hours-factor

Variable Cafe AM Bar PM Sun–Wed Bar PM Thu–Sat
Active seats 100 100 125
Avg turns per seat per operating block 1.4 1.2 1.8
Avg check per turn 160K 280K 390K
Operating hours in block 6 9 9
Revenue per block 22.4M 33.6M 87.8M

Note: avg check per turn (390K) is lower than the 700K "avg check" in the plan because a single guest typically occupies a seat for one turn (~1h45m) — the plan's 700K implies a full evening's spend per seat, which is only true for 20–30% of guests (multi-drink + plates + tasting).

Realization factors

Theoretical ≠ actual. Stack realization losses:

Factor % hit Reason
Ramp factor Y1 −20% Under steady-state; 6-month ramp per advisory
Seasonality (HCMC) −8% Slow Jan–Feb; strong Nov–Dec
Weather (rooftop) −5% Monsoon hits ~30% of May–Oct days
No-shows + walk-outs −3% Industry standard
Discount + comp −2% Opening promotions + comps
Blended realization ~63%

Simulated Y1 monthly: 2,400M × 0.63 = ~1,512M Simulated Y1 annual: ~18,000M before the ramp-profile distribution

But Y1 is back-loaded because of the ramp. The shape: - Months 1–3: 50% of steady-state = ~750M/mo - Months 4–6: 70% = ~1,060M/mo - Months 7–9: 85% = ~1,280M/mo - Months 10–12: 100% = ~1,510M/mo

Annual Y1 simulated total: (750×3 + 1060×3 + 1280×3 + 1510×3) = 13,800M

Which interestingly matches the model's 13.83B number — but only if you accept: - 63% blended realization - 6-month ramp to 85% - Reaching steady state by month 10

If steady state slips to month 12+ (advisory base case): ~12,500M — matching the advisory's figure.

Sensitivity table

Scenario Assumption change Y1 revenue
Base (as above) — 13,800M
Slow ramp (+3 mo) Steady state month 13 12,500M
Check-size disappointment Thu–Sat check 340K instead of 390K 12,000M
Slow ramp + check-size Both of above 10,800M
Both + cafe underperforms Cafe AM at 70% of plan 10,100M
Upside Ramp fast + 420K check 15,400M

What the simulation tells us

  1. 13.83B is achievable under base-case realization + the model's intended ramp. It is not a "stretch" scenario — it is the expected case.
  2. 12–13B is the honest Y1 range. Anything below is structural failure (check-size, ramp, or cafe).
  3. The 700K avg check is not the right question. The right question is the 390K per-turn check. That is what monthly menu engineering should target.
  4. 6-month ramp is the pivot assumption. It changes EBITDA timing materially and should be the scenario shown to investors.
