Report 04 — HR Ramp-Up

Parent: Home · Source: 04_operations/reports/04_hr_rampup.html · Related: ../Shifts, ../OpEx, 01_Risk-Analysis

Why this matters

Over-hiring in the first 60 days is the single most common failure mode in F&B. Under-hiring at opening damages service quality and reviews. The ramp plan below phases hiring to revenue, not to headcount targets.

Hiring waves

Wave 1 — April (pre-construction, this week)

Purpose: leadership + physical-build support

Hire Timing Why now
GM Already in role Owns construction + licensing
Kitchen Lead 2026-04-22 Menu finalization + kitchen spec sign-off
Bar Lead 2026-04-25 Cocktail program development + equipment sign-off

Wave 2 — Mid-May (T-4 weeks)

Purpose: core operations team

Hire Timing Why
Supervisor 2026-05-11 Support GM during final build + licensing push
Service Lead 2026-05-15 Service standards design + hiring panel
Bartender 1 2026-05-18 Bar program implementation
Commis 2026-05-20 Kitchen prep development

Wave 3 — Early June (T-2 weeks)

Purpose: full team pre-open

Hire Timing
Bartender 2 2026-06-01
Server 1 2026-06-02
Server 2 2026-06-04
Cashier 2026-06-04
Security 2026-06-06
Maintenance 2026-06-08
Housekeeping 1 2026-06-08
Housekeeping 2 2026-06-08

Wave 4 — Opening + first 30 days

Purpose: part-time flex, adjust based on actuals

  • Part-time pool (~4 FTE equivalent) hired rolling, Jun 10 onward
  • Adjust pool size based on Thu–Sat actuals at week 4 and week 8

Training plan

Training block Duration Who
Welcome + brand + values 1 day Everyone
Service standards 2 days FOH
Coffee program 3 days Baristas + servers (cross-trained)
Cocktail program — classics 3 days Bar team
Cocktail program — signatures 3 days Bar team
Kitchen stations 5 days Kitchen team
Sound Lab protocols 1 day Bar Lead + Service Lead
POS + reservations 1 day FOH + cashier
PCCC + safety 1 day Everyone
ATVSTP food safety 1 day Kitchen + FOH handling food
Soft-open rehearsal 2 nights Full team

Pre-open training budget: ~30–40M (included in pre-opening 250M).

Labor cost ramp

Month Wave Headcount Monthly labor cost
Apr 2026 (partial) Wave 1 3 ~25M
May 2026 + Wave 2 7 ~125M
Jun 2026 (pre-open) + Wave 3 15 ~240M
Jun 2026 (post-open) + Wave 4 pool 18 FTE ~330M
Jul–Aug 2026 Full 18 FTE ~336M

Cumulative pre-opening labor: ~390M (counted against 250M pre-opening budget + early-month working capital).

Retention levers

  • Profit-share for Bar Lead + Kitchen Lead — tied to monthly EBITDA above threshold
  • Two-off-per-week rule — see ../Shifts
  • Published schedule 3 weeks ahead — local industry best practice
  • Quarterly team trip + training budget
  • Internal promotion path — supervisor → manager → area manager

Attrition expectations

Industry base rate for F&B line staff in HCMC: ~80–100% annualized. Target: keep below 60% in Y1 through the levers above.

Critical hire retention target: 100% of Bar Lead + Kitchen Lead through month 12. Losing either is a top-5 risk (01_Risk-Analysis #5).
