Report 02 — Revenue Diversification

Parent: Home · Source: 04_operations/reports/02_revenue_diversification.html · Related: ../Revenue-Plan, 01_Risk-Analysis

The concentration problem

CUR8's plan generates ~60% of weekly revenue from Thu–Sat PM alone. That is a single concentration of risk: - 3 days - 1 daypart - 1 customer segment (PM bar crowd)

Any disruption to this 18-hour window per week (e.g. major event, rain, illness in bar team) puts a meaningful chunk of monthly revenue in jeopardy.

Diversification axes

Axis 1 — Daypart

Daypart % of week Diversification grade
AM cafe Sun–Wed 13% F — too low
AM cafe Thu–Sat 14% C — okay
PM bar Sun–Wed 20% B
PM bar Thu–Sat 53% F (concentration)

Segment % of revenue Diversification grade

Creative regulars 15% B
Remote workers 10% B
Sound Lab members 10% A
Rooftop weekend 10% C
Walk-in / tourist 5% A

Diversification interventions (priority order)

Quick wins (Q1 post-open)

  1. Cafe loyalty program — 10-stamp card, push AM Sun–Wed bills from 120 to 150
  2. Wednesday Sound Lab session — pulls revenue earlier in the week
  3. Group / corporate after-work package — books PM Sun–Wed via outbound sales
  4. Rooftop happy hour — 17:00–19:00 Mon–Wed, builds destination habit

Medium-term (Q2–Q3)

  1. Vinyl club membership — 50–80 paying members at 500K/mo = recurring 25–40M
  2. Private hire / event channel — 1–2 events/month, 25–60M each
  3. Brunch program Sat–Sun — extends AM revenue
  4. Coffee retail (whole bean takeaway + subscription) — incremental ~5M/month at start

Strategic (Q4 +)

  1. Second-room programming — Sound Lab afternoon listening sessions for cafe segment
  2. Off-site bar program — pop-up at events; brand spread, low capex
