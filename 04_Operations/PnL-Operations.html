The operations source page records "1.215B rev · 808M net (monthly ops view)". That 808M figure is gross profit before fixed opex — i.e. revenue minus only direct cost of sales — useful for daily/weekly venue dashboards but not the full bottom line.

Reconciling 808M vs 284M

View What it captures Number
Daily venue dashboard view (revenue minus COGS minus direct labor) What the GM watches every day ~808M
Full operating profit (after rent + utilities + admin + tax) What the investor watches monthly ~284M

Both are correct; they answer different questions.

Cash conversion

  • 1.215B revenue → ~284M monthly net cash → ~3.4B annualized
  • Compare to 5-year model NPAT Y1: 1.79B → significantly higher because the 5-year model assumes ramp + lower realization in Y1
  • Reconciliation: the steady-state monthly view assumes Y2-equivalent volumes; Y1 model averages 60–70% of this

Sensitivities

Lever Impact on monthly net
Revenue −10% −175M (cash net halves)
COGS +2pp −24M
Labor +10% (over-hire) −34M
Rent +20% (Y3 step) −15M
Utilities summer premium −5M (Apr–Oct only)

What protects the bottom line

  1. Hold COGS at 30% — bar lead's most important KPI
  2. Hold labor at 28% — schedule discipline, see Shifts
  3. Don't expand the menu without margin math — Menu
  4. Don't take group discounts that erode the Thu–Sat avg check
