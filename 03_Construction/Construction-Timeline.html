Critical path items

  1. Sound Lab acoustic build-out (weeks 4–5) — slip pushes everything
  2. PCCC sign-off (multiple touchpoints; final sign-off blocks opening)
  3. Joinery delivery (week 6) — long lead-time custom bar
  4. AV commissioning (week 8) — Sound Lab tuning needs 3 days minimum
  5. License live dates (parallel; see 04_Operations/Licenses)

Risk to the schedule

Risk Probability Impact Mitigation
PCCC change orders High 1–2 weeks Pre-engage PCCC consultant week 1; design fire system to spec, not minimum
Joinery late delivery Medium 1 week Order joinery now (week 1), penalty clause in PO
Acoustic engineer change request Medium 1 week Lock acoustic spec week 2; no late-stage changes
Hidden structural condition Medium 2 weeks Early structural survey (week 1)
Heavy rain (May–Jun monsoon onset) Medium 0.5 week Rooftop scope last; weatherproof temporary
Equipment delivery delay Low 1 week Equipment ordered now; ETA confirmed

Contingency

The 8-week schedule has 2.5 days of slack built in (Jun 15–17 buffer before Jun 18 open). That is thin. If any one of the high/medium-impact risks materializes, opening slips into late June.

Recommended: the GM should plan for a soft open Jun 13 (invite-only) and hard open Jun 18, so the buffer is operational rather than purely calendar.
